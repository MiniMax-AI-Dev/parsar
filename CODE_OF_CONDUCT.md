# Code of Conduct

Parsar follows the [Contributor Covenant 2.1][cov]. By participating in this
project — issues, pull requests, code review, discussions — you agree to
abide by it.

## In short

We pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression,
level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality,
personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

### Examples of behaviour we want

- Empathy and kindness toward other people
- Respect for differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Constructive feedback, gracefully given and gracefully received
- Taking responsibility, apologising to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the
  overall community

### Examples we will not tolerate

- Sexualised language or imagery, or sexual attention or advances of any
  kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political
  attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Reporting

Report unacceptable behaviour to the maintainers via a
[private GitHub Security Advisory][advisory] — this gives us a confidential
channel without exposing the reporter. All complaints will be reviewed and
investigated promptly and fairly.

Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident.

## Enforcement

The full text of Contributor Covenant 2.1 — including the enforcement
ladder (correction → warning → temporary ban → permanent ban) — applies
verbatim. See <https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/>.
